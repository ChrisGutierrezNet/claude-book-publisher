# Voice & Style Analysis

## Target Voice for Business Books

- Conversational but professional (peer over coffee, not a lecture)
- First person where appropriate ("I built this, here's what I learned")
- Active voice, strong verbs, short paragraphs
- Stories and examples BEFORE abstractions
- Direct and confident without being arrogant

## Anti-Patterns

### Passive Voice

- **Bad:** "The report was generated by the AI"
- **Good:** "The AI generated the report"
- **Target:** <15% passive sentences

### Hedging Language

Undermines authority. Flag these:

- "might", "could possibly", "perhaps", "it seems"
- "somewhat", "arguably", "to some extent"
- "in my opinion" (you wrote the book, it's all your opinion)
- "I think/believe/feel that" (just state it)

### Guru-Speak

Makes readers cringe. Flag these:

- "unlock your potential", "unleash the power"
- "transform/revolutionize your business"
- "game-changer", "paradigm shift"
- "synergy", "leverage" (as verb)
- "disrupt", "empower", "supercharge"
- "10x", "exponential" (when not literal)

### Filler Phrases

Add nothing. Flag these:

- "It's important to note that..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "The reality is that..."
- "Let's be honest..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "The fact of the matter is..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Needless to say..."

### Textbook Voice

- "In this chapter, we will explore..."
- "As previously mentioned..."
- "The reader should note that..."
- "It can be concluded that..."

## Readability Metrics

### Sentence Length

- **Short:** 1-8 words (punchy, emphatic)
- **Medium:** 9-20 words (standard)
- **Long:** 21-35 words (complex ideas)
- **Too long:** 36+ words (needs splitting)
- **Ideal mix:** 20% short, 60% medium, 20% long

### Paragraph Length

- **Ideal:** 2-4 sentences
- **Acceptable:** 1-6 sentences
- **Flag:** 7+ sentences

### Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

- **Target:** 8-10 (accessible to college-educated professionals)
- **Warning:** 12+ (too academic)
- **Warning:** <6 (too simplistic)
